About

Ari Ofengenden teaches at Tulane University and has thought, taught, and written about ideology and the relations between ideas and power for upwards of twenty years. He has written numerous articles on Jewish-German writers, psychoanalysis, discourse theory, capitalism, and culture.

He earned an M.A. in cognitive psychology, a Ph.D. in comparative literature from Haifa University, and did post-doctoral work at Tübingen University. He has taught at George Washington University and Oberlin College. He is an Elected Member of the European Academy of Sciences and Arts.
